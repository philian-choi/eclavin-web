# Episode 093: Final Mock Exam 3 (The Tannin Scale) - Integrated Review

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following correctly lists red grape varieties in order from Low to High tannin?

A. Cabernet Sauvignon - Syrah - Pinot Noir
B. Pinot Noir - Merlot - Cabernet Sauvignon
C. Syrah - Pinot Noir - Cabernet Sauvignon
D. Cabernet Sauvignon - Pinot Noir - Merlot

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Pinot Noir - Merlot - Cabernet Sauvignon

Explanation:
- Pinot Noir: Thin skin, lowest tannin, pale color.
- Merlot: Medium tannin, velvety texture, plum flavors.
- Cabernet Sauvignon: Thick skin, highest tannin, deep color, high acidity.

---

## [Expert Concept]

The Red Wine Hierarchy:

1.  Pinot Noir: Light body, High acid, Low tannin. (The Elegant)
2.  Merlot: Medium body, Medium tannin. (The Balanced)
3.  Cabernet Sauvignon / Syrah: Full body, High tannin. (The Powerful)

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Don't confuse color with quality. Pinot Noir is pale but can be one of the most expensive wines in the world.
[Tip]: Memorize the "Tannin Ladder": Pinot (Low) -> Merlot (Mid) -> Cabernet (High).
