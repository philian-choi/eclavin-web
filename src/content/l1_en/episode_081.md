# Episode 081: Sweet Wine Temperature (The Chill Factor) - Service Mastery

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. What is the most appropriate serving temperature for very sweet wines like Sauternes or Tokaji?

A. Room Temperature (18°C)
B. Lightly Chilled (12°C)
C. Well-chilled (6~8°C)
D. Warm (40°C)

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: C. Well-chilled (6~8°C)

Explanation: Because sweet wines have intense sugar levels, they can feel cloying and sticky if served too warm. Serving them 'Well-chilled' (6~8°C) cleans up the sweetness and highlights the fresh acidity, creating the best balance. Sweet and Sparkling wines are the categories that should be served the coldest.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Serving temperature guide by style:

1.  Sweet Wine: Well-chilled (6~8°C). (Coldest to balance sugar)
2.  Sparkling Wine: Well-chilled (6~10°C). (To preserve bubbles and freshness)
3.  Light White: Chilled (7~10°C). (To emphasize zestiness)
4.  Full White: Lightly Chilled (10~13°C). (To allow complex flavors to show)

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Don't think "it's expensive, so it should be served at room temp to smell it better." Sweet wines lose their charm as they warm up.
[Tip]: If an exam asks for the temperature of 'Sweet wine' or 'Dessert wine,' the keyword is 'Well-chilled.'
