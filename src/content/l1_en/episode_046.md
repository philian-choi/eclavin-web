# Episode 046: Wine Storage Principles (The Preservation - Final Prep)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. When storing wine sealed with a cork for a long period, what is the most fundamental reason it must be stored horizontally (lying down)?

A. To prevent the wine bottle from rolling.
B. To keep the cork moist with wine, preventing it from drying out and letting air penetrate.
C. To artificially increase the alcohol content of the wine.
D. To make the color of the wine darker.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. To keep the cork moist with wine, preventing it from drying out and letting air penetrate.

Explanation: Cork is a natural material made from tree bark. 

If the bottle is stood upright, the cork dries out and shrinks, and excessive oxygen enters through the gap, oxidizing the wine. 

Therefore, wines for long-term storage must be stored lying down. 

On the other hand, wines with a screw cap can be stood upright without any issues.

---

## [Expert Concept]

These are the 4 conditions for the best 'haven' for wine.

1.  Temperature: A constant temperature between 10-15°C is best. Places where the temperature fluctuates (e.g., a kitchen) induce expansion and contraction of the wine, ruining its quality.
2.  Light: Direct sunlight or strong lighting shortens the life of the wine. Store it in a dark place.
3.  Position: Lay corked wines horizontally. (Maintains cork humidity!)
4.  Stillness: Place it in a place without vibrations so the wine can mature slowly without stress.

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Thinking "It's perfect to store it in the refrigerator all year round" is incorrect. 
A regular refrigerator has too low a temperature, is too dry, and has frequent vibrations, making it unsuitable for long-term storage.
[Tip]: In the exam, connect the 'Moist cork' and 'Horizontal' keywords!
