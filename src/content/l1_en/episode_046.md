# Episode 046: Wine Storage Principles (The Preservation - Final Prep)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. When storing wine sealed with a cork for a long period, what is the most fundamental reason for storing the wine horizontally (lying down)?

A. To prevent the wine bottle from rolling away.
B. To keep the cork moist at all times, preventing it from drying out and allowing air to seep in.
C. To artificially increase the alcohol content of the wine.
D. To make the color of the wine deeper.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. To keep the cork moist at all times, preventing it from drying out and allowing air to seep in.

Explanation: Cork is a natural component made from tree bark.

If a bottle is stood up, the cork becomes dry and shrinks in volume, allowing excessive oxygen to enter through the gap and oxidize the wine.

Therefore, wines intended for long-term storage must be stored horizontally.

On the other hand, screw cap wines can be stored standing up without any issues.

---

## [Expert Concept - Final Prep]

These are the four conditions for the best 'haven' for wine:

1.  Temperature: A constant temperature between 10-15°C is best. Places where the temperature fluctuates (e.g., the kitchen) induce expansion and contraction of the wine, ruining its quality.

2.  Light: Direct sunlight or strong lighting shortens the life of the wine. Store it in a dark place.

3.  Position: Lie cork-sealed wines down horizontally. (Maintain cork humidity!)

4.  Stillness (Stability): Keep it in a place without vibration so that the wine can age slowly without stress.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid thinking that "storing it in the refrigerator all year round is perfect."
General refrigerators are too cold and dry, and have frequent vibrations, making them unsuitable for long-term storage.
[Tip]: In an exam, connect 'Moist cork' and 'Horizontal' keywords!
