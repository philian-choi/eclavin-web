# Episode 050: Final Check - Wine Service & Ethics

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. During the wine service process, what is the most appropriate first thing to check before pouring wine for a customer?

A. Check if the color of the wine is pretty.
B. Check if the wine bottle label matches the wine ordered by the customer.
C. Smell the cork first.
D. Check if the snacks (appetizers) are ready.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Check if the wine bottle label matches the wine ordered by the customer.

Explanation: The foundation and core of wine service is 'accuracy.'

In order to prevent the mistake of the served wine being different from the wine expected by the customer, it is essential to politely show the label to the customer and get confirmation before opening the bottle.

This is also an important first step for building trust.

---

## [Expert Concept - Final Check]

The three major principles for perfect wine service are as follows:

1.  Verification (Label Check): Confirm that the order details and the actual bottle match right in front of the customer's eyes.

2.  Fault Checking: Pour a very small amount of wine for the customer and have them check the condition through tasting to see if there are any problems with the aroma (e.g., corked or not).

3.  Safe Service: A dignified attitude is required, pouring politely without touching the rim of the glass or covering the label.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Some people think that "covering the label with your hand during service is polite," but it is exactly the opposite. The label should always face the front or top so that the customer can see it clearly.

[Tip]: The WSET exam focuses on the 'system' rather than 'dignity.' Visualize this sequence in your head like a machine: Order confirmation -> Cork removal -> Fault check -> Service.
