A. Intense tropical fruit like pineapple.
B. **Savory notes of biscuit, bread, and toast.**
C. Spicy notes of black pepper and cloves.
D. Deep vegetative aromas like asparagus.

**Q2. When assessing the 'Appearance' of a wine during a formal tasting, what is the professional's primary focus?**

A. The design and price of the bottle.
B. **The clarity and health of the wine (identifying potential faults).**
C. The length of the aftertaste (finish).
D. The amount of sugar added to the blend.

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answers: Q1: B. Biscuit/Bread/Toast | Q2: B. Clarity & Health.**

**Explanation**: 1. **Champagne** undergoes secondary fermentation in the bottle, where dead yeast cells (lees) release **Autolytic flavors** like bread and biscuit—a hallmark of quality. 2. A professional begins every tasting with the **EYE**. **Clarity** is the first indicator of a wine's health; haziness or excessive brown tints (in youthful whites) can signal oxidation or faults.
