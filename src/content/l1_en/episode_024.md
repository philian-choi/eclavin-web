# Episode 024: Riesling (Aroma & Sugar Balance)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

**Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of the Riesling grape variety?**

A. It is always produced in a bone-dry style.
B. It is characterized by very high acidity and elegant floral and fruit aromas.
C. It must be grown in warm regions to reach its full potential.
D. Vanilla flavors from oak aging are its most essential characteristic.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: B. It is characterized by very high acidity and elegant floral and fruit aromas.**

**Explanation**: Riesling is a highly **aromatic** variety, offering rich scents of apple, peach, lime, and elegant floral notes (honeysuckle). Thanks to its **very high acidity**, it can maintain a beautiful balance across a wide range of styles, from bone-dry to lusciously sweet.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Key characteristics of Riesling (Essential for WSET L1):

1.  **High Acidity**: This is the backbone that maintains freshness and balances sweetness in dessert styles.
2.  **Aromatic**: Distinctive aromas include green apple, lime, peach, and apricot.
3.  **Range of Sweetness**: Produced in a wide spectrum, including Dry, Medium-dry, and Sweet.
4.  **Maturation**: Oak aging is rarely used, as it would mask the pure, vibrant primary aromas of the grape.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not assume that "Riesling is always sweet." Many regions, such as parts of Germany, Alsace, and Australia, produce world-class dry Rieslings.
**[Tip]**: When you see **'Riesling'** on the exam, immediately think of **'Aromatic'** and **'High Acidity'**. This acidic balance is what allows the aromas to shine. This is a core concept for a perfect score.
