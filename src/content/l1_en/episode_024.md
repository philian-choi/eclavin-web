# Episode 024: Riesling (The Aromatic Queen) - White Grape Variety

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of the typical characteristics of the Riesling grape?

A. Very low acidity and high tannin, and strong oak aromas like a white wine.
B. High acidity and intense aromatic character, with flavors of lime and peach.
C. No aroma at all, and always produced locally as a low-cost wine by injecting carbon dioxide gas.
D. High sugar content, and always produced as a deep brown sweet wine.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. High acidity and intense aromatic character, with flavors of lime and peach.

Explanation: Riesling is so elegant and precise that it's called the 'Queen of White Wines.'

Its high acidity gives it an extremely fresh and clean sensation on the palate, and its citrus-family aromas such as lime, lemon, and green apple blend harmoniously with stone fruits such as peach and apricot.

Specifically, as it ages, it can develop a unique aroma called 'Petrol' (petroleum).

---

## [Expert Concept]

Riesling is synonymous with 'Purity and Long-term Aging Power':

1.  High Acidity: Acts as a backbone that prevents the wine from becoming cloying, even if it has a high sugar content.

2.  Aromatic Profile:
    - Fruit: Lime, Lemon, Green apple, Peach, Apricot.
    - Tertiary: Petrol (A unique aroma that appears with aging).

3.  Sweetness Range: Produced in a wide range of styles, from extremely Dry to very Sweet.

4.  Unoaked Style: To preserve the delicate aromas and acidity of the grape, it is rarely matured in oak.

5.  Major Regions: Germany (Mosel, Rheingau), France (Alsace), Australia (Clare Valley, Eden Valley), etc.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid the assumption that "All Riesling is sweet."
German 'Trocken' and Australian Rieslings are in a very crisp, Dry style.
[Tip]: If the words 'Lime' or 'High Acidity' appear in the exam, always choose 'Riesling.'
The autobahn of acidity and sugar is only for Riesling.
