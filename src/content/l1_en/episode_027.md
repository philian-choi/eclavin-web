# Episode 027: Tempranillo (The Pride of Spain) - Black Grape Variety

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of the typical characteristics of the Tempranillo grape?

A. Thin skins and very low tannin, produced always as a white wine.
B. Medium tannin and acidity, with strawberry and red cherry aromas, and leather notes when matured in oak.
C. No aroma at all, and always grown in very cold Arctic regions.
D. High sugar content, and always produced as a sparkling wine.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Medium tannin and acidity, with strawberry and red cherry aromas, and leather notes when matured in oak.

Explanation: Tempranillo is the 'national representative' variety of Spain.

It has medium-thick skins, resulting in perfectly balanced medium levels of tannin and acidity, with a foundation of bright red fruit aromas like strawberry and red cherry.

Notably, as it undergoes oak and bottle maturation, complex flavors like vanilla, cloves, and leather are added.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Tempranillo showcases the 'Aesthetics of Maturation':

1.  Medium Balance: Tannin, acidity, and body are all at medium levels, showing excellent harmony.

2.  Aromatic Profile: Strawberry, Red cherry, Plum, Vanilla (oak), Leather (maturation).

3.  Oak Affinity: It highly prefers oak maturation, dividing into various grades like 'Rioja' wines depending on the maturation period.

4.  Major Regions: Rioja, Ribera del Duero (Spain).

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid thinking that "Tempranillo only tastes like freshly harvested grapes."
The 'Vanilla' from oak and 'Leather' from maturation are the true charm points of this variety.
[Tip]: In an exam, if words like 'Strawberry', 'Leather', and 'Spain' appear, always choose 'Tempranillo.'
The soul of Spain lies in Tempranillo.
