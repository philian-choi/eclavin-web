# Episode 001: Still, Sparkling, & Fortified - The Three Pillars of Wine

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

**Q. Which type of wine is made by adding a separate spirit (alcohol) to increase the alcohol content and enhance preservation?**

A. Still Wine
B. Sparkling Wine
C. **Fortified Wine**
D. Light Wine

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: C. Fortified Wine**

**Explanation**: Wine is more than just a drink made from grapes. It is a scientific product of the sugars in **Vitis vinifera** (European grape species) meeting yeast and transforming into alcohol. **Fortified Wine**, in particular, is characterized by the intentional addition of a neutral spirit (such as brandy) before, during, or after fermentation, raising the alcohol content to between 15% and 22% ABV. Famous examples include Port and Sherry.

---

## [Expert Concept]

According to WSET Level 1, wines are broadly categorized into three main types (Types):

1.  **Still Wine**: This is a normal wine with no bubbles (carbon dioxide). The vast majority of wines fall into this category, with an alcohol content typically between 8% and 15% ABV. (e.g., Chablis, Sancerre)
2.  **Sparkling Wine**: A wine that has trapped carbon dioxide generated during fermentation inside the bottle, creating bubbles. (e.g., Champagne, Prosecco, Cava)
3.  **Fortified Wine**: A wine that has had high-proof alcohol (Spirit) added to it to increase its shelf life or specifically for unique flavors. (e.g., Port, Sherry)

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Many students mistakenly think Champagne is 'fortified' because it is perceived as potent. However, the defining characteristic of Champagne is its 'bubbles', making it a **Sparkling Wine**.
**[Tip]**: The word 'Fortified' inherently means 'made stronger by adding alcohol'. If you see words like 'Spirit' or 'Extra Alcohol' in the exam, the correct answer is unquestionably **Fortified Wine**.
