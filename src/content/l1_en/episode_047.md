# Episode 047: Social Responsibility Recap (Final Prep - Drinking Wisely)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

**Q. According to World Health Organization (WHO) and government guidelines, what is considered the safest approach regarding alcohol consumption during pregnancy?**

A. One glass of red wine per day is healthy.
B. Drinking alcohol is fine as long as it's paired with food.
C. **Avoiding alcohol completely is the safest choice**.
D. Only drinking distilled spirits, and avoiding wine.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: C. Avoiding alcohol completely is the safest choice.**

**Explanation**: 1. **Pregnancy**: The safest option is total abstinence from alcohol to avoid any risk to the fetus. 2. **Responsible Drinking**: Use guidelines from your local government or health bodies. 3. **The Liver**: The liver is the primary organ that processes alcohol at a rate of approximately one unit (about 10g of pure ethanol) per hour. 4. **Safety**: Never operate a vehicle or heavy machinery after consuming alcohol, as it impairs coordination and reaction time.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Ethics are the 'Foundations' of the Wine Culture.

1.  **Metabolism**: Alcohol processing takes time. Water and food do *not* speed up the liver's processing of ethanol. Patience is the only way to sober up.
2.  **Health Guidelines**: These are designed to minimize long-term health risks (liver disease, addiction, etc.). Always stay within the recommended daily or weekly limits.
3.  **Community Responsibility**: A true wine enthusiast respects the drink and the safety of those around them. This includes knowing the minimum legal drinking age in your country and adhering to it.
4.  **Moderation**: Savor the quality rather than the quantity—the hallmark of a professional approach to wine.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not think coffee or a cold shower will help you sober up faster for an exam question! Only **'Time'** allows the liver to process alcohol.
**[Tip]**: In a L1 exam, **'Total Abstinence'** is always the correct stance regarding **'Pregnancy'**.
