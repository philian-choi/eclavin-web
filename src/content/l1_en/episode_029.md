# Episode 029: Grenache (Alcohol & Ripe Fruit)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

**Q. Which of the following is a typical characteristic of the Grenache grape variety?**

A. Very high acidity and very low alcohol content
B. Low acidity, high alcohol content (High Alcohol), and ripe red fruit flavors
C. A transparent white wine with zero tannins
D. A cool-climate variety grown exclusively in Germany

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: B. Low acidity, high alcohol content (High Alcohol), and ripe red fruit flavors**

**Explanation**: Grenache grapes accumulate sugar efficiently, leading to **high alcohol content** upon fermentation. The acidity is relatively low, making the wine feel round and soft on the palate. Its profile is dominated by sweet, ripe red fruit flavors like strawberry and raspberry, and it is also frequently used for rosé production (e.g., Tavel).

---

## [Expert Concept]

Key characteristics of Grenache (Essential for WSET L1):

1.  **Low Acidity**: Results in a soft, smooth, and round mouthfeel.
2.  **High Alcohol**: Due to its ability to reach high sugar levels, the final alcohol content is typically elevated.
3.  **Red Fruit Flavors**: Dominated by sweet strawberry and raspberry aromas.
4.  **Regions**: Primarily grown in the warm regions of the Southern Rhône in France and Spain (where it is known as Garnacha).

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not attribute "black fruit flavors" to Grenache. Those are the hallmarks of varieties like Syrah or Cabernet Sauvignon.
**[Tip]**: When the exam mentions **'Grenache'**, immediately associate it with the keywords **'High Alcohol'** and **'Red Fruit'**. This warm, red profile is essential for a 30/30 score.
