# Episode 029: Other Notable Red Varieties (The New World Powerhouses) - Black Grape Varieties

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which red wine variety matches the following description? 
"It has thick skins and high tannin, and when grown in very warm climates, it exhibits very high alcohol along with flavors of dried plum and black pepper."

A. Zinfandel / Primitivo
B. Pinot Noir
C. Sauvignon Blanc
D. Riesling

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: A. Zinfandel / Primitivo

Explanation: Zinfandel is a variety that ripens very well under the hot sun.

Its high sugar content ferments into high alcohol, and it is characterized by intense black fruit flavors like dried plum (raisin) or jam, along with spicy black pepper notes.

In Italy, it is known as 'Primitivo.'

---

## [Expert Concept]

These individualistic red varieties each have their own signature aromas and structures:

1.  Zinfandel (Primitivo):
    - Characteristics: High alcohol, Full body. (Concentrated sun)
    - Aroma: Black cherry, Dried plum, Black pepper.

2.  Malbec:
    - Characteristics: Full body, Medium-High Tannin. (The heart of Argentina)
    - Aroma: Blackberry, Black plum, Violet.

3.  Pinotage:
    - Characteristics: High Tannin, Medium-High Acidity. (The pride of South Africa)
    - Aroma: Red berry, Coffee, Smoke, Earth.

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Avoid thinking that "Malbec is always light and soft."
Malbec is a masculine style with a very deep color and heavy body.
[Tip]: In an exam, if the words 'Black Pepper' and 'High Alcohol' appear together, always choose 'Zinfandel.' It is the undisputed King of California.
