# Episode 020: Closures

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

**Q. What is considered one of the most significant advantages of a wine sealed with a screwcap (Screwcap)?**

A. It allows more oxygen to enter the wine, which aids in its maturation.
B. It provides protection against 'cork taint (TCA)'.
C. It helps increase the final alcohol content of the wine.
D. It must always be stored in a vertical position to maintain its seal.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: B. It provides protection against 'cork taint (TCA)'.**

**Explanation**: Traditional cork closures allow the wine to breathe through microscopic gaps, but they can occasionally lead to **cork taint (Cork Taint)**. This is caused by a chemical compound called **TCA (Trichloroanisole)**, which makes the wine smell like damp cardboard or mold. In contrast, **screwcaps** provide a very tight seal and completely eliminate the risk of such cork contamination, ensuring the wine tastes exactly as the winemaker intended.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Core knowledge of wine closures as defined by WSET Level 1:

1.  **Corks**:
    - Advantages: Preferred for wines intended for long-term aging, as the minute ingress of oxygen helps develop complex tertiary flavors over time.
    - Disadvantages: Risk of cork taint (TCA) and potentially crumbling if the cork dries out; bottles must be stored horizontally to keep the cork moist.
2.  **Screwcaps**:
    - Advantages: Extremely effective at sealing the bottle, preserving fruit-forward freshness (highly favored in the New World). There is zero risk of cork taint, and bottles can be stored in any orientation.
    - Disadvantages: Because they block almost all oxygen, they are less suitable for wines that require oxygen to mature over many decades.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not fall into the trap of thinking that "Screwcaps are only used for cheap, low-quality wines." Many premium winemakers around the world now choose screwcaps to ensure consistency and quality.
**[Tip]**: In the exam, if 'Screwcap' is mentioned, prioritize keywords like **'Preservation of fresh fruit flavor'** or **'No risk of cork taint.'** This distinction is vital for achieving a perfect 30/30 score.
