# Episode 026: Grenache / Garnacha (The Sun Worshipper) - Black Grape Variety

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of the typical characteristics of the Grenache grape?

A. Thick skins, very high tannin, and fresh lemon aromas.
B. Thin skins, low to medium tannin and acidity, with high alcohol and ripe strawberry flavors.
C. No aroma at all, and always grown in very cold Arctic regions.
D. Always deep purple in color, produced only as a sweet wine with high sugar content.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Thin skins, low to medium tannin and acidity, with high alcohol and ripe strawberry flavors.

Explanation: Grenache is a variety that loves the sun.

Because of its thin skins, it has a pale color and relatively low tannin and acidity, but it accumulates a lot of sugar in warm climates, resulting in high alcohol levels.

Typical aromas include ripe red strawberry and red plum, along with subtle hints of white pepper.

It is also widely used in the production of high-quality rosé wines.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Grenache is synonymous with 'Warm Sunshine and Sweetness':

1.  High Alcohol & Full Body: In warm climates, the high sugar content of the grapes creates wines with higher ABV (Alcohol by Volume) and a plump body.

2.  Aromatic Profile: Ripe strawberry, Red plum, White pepper, Spices.

3.  Rosé Production: Due to its thin skins and rich fruit flavors, it is frequently used to make premium rosés.

4.  Blending Partner: Often blended with Syrah or Mourvèdre to add tannin or acidity (e.g., GSM blends in the Rhône Valley, France).

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid assuming that "Grenache is always deep in color and very astringent."
Its thin skin means it's usually paler in color and softer in tannin.
[Tip]: If the words 'Strawberry' and 'High Alcohol' appear together in an exam, always choose 'Grenache/Garnacha.'
The sweetness of the sun is the indicator of Grenache.
