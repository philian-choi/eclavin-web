# Episode 058: Syrah / Shiraz (The Spice of Life) - Characteristics

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. What is the most unique and iconic flavor found in wines made from the Syrah / Shiraz grape?

A. Fresh apple and pear.
B. Black fruit and spice flavors like Black Pepper.
C. Subtle white flower and peach.
D. Butter and vanilla.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Black fruit and spice flavors like Black Pepper.

Explanation: Syrah is a bold variety characterized by intense black fruit (blackberry, black cherry) and a distinct note of 'Black Pepper' that tingles the nose. In the Northern Rhône (France), it’s called 'Syrah' and is made in an elegant, peppery style. In warmer regions like Australia, it’s called 'Shiraz' and is made into a richer, sweeter, and more full-bodied style.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Syrah is a wine of 'Power' and 'Flavor':

1.  Structure: High Tannin, Full Body, Medium Acidity.
2.  Flavors: Black Fruit (Blackberry) + Spice (Black Pepper, Liquorice).
3.  Naming: Syrah (French style, elegant) vs. Shiraz (Australian style, bold).
4.  Oak: It often undergoes oak ageing to add notes of chocolate or smoke to its powerful profile.

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Don't think "Shiraz is a light white wine." It is one of the darkest and most full-bodied red wines.
[Tip]: If 'Black Pepper', 'Blackberry', and 'Full Body' are combined in an exam question, the answer is 'Syrah / Shiraz.'
