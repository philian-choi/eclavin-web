# Episode 045: Glassware & Service Etiquette (The Tools for the Job - Final Prep)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. What shape of glass is recommended for appreciating and maintaining the fine bubbles of sparkling wine for the longest time?

A. Red wine glass with a large round bowl.
B. Tall and narrow Flute glass.
C. Wide and shallow Coupe glass.
D. A large beer mug without a handle.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Tall and narrow Flute glass.

Explanation: Flute glasses minimize the surface area in contact with air, slowing down the rate at which carbon dioxide escapes.

They also visually display the beautiful process of bubbles rising from the bottom to the top and are effective in maintaining a cool temperature.

In contrast, red wine glasses have a wide opening, which is better for spreading the aroma.

---

## [Expert Concept - Final Prep]

A wine glass is not just a container, but a 'tool' that conveys flavor.

1.  Red Wine Glass: The largest size. Widens the oxygen contact area to soften harsh tannins and gather gorgeous aromas.

2.  White Wine Glass: Smaller than a red glass or medium size. Protects the temperature from rising too quickly and maintains delicate fruit aromas.

3.  Service Etiquette: 
    - Stem Control: You should grab the stem or the base instead of the bowl. This prevents fingerprints and keeps body heat from transferring to the wine and changing its taste.
    - Cleanliness: If there is a detergent smell or fishy water smell from the glass, it completely ruins the wine aroma. Perfectly remove moisture with dry linen.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid thinking that "all wine glasses must be expensive."
'Cleanliness (No odors)' and 'Glass shape' are much more important than a high price.
[Tip]: In an exam, if 'Tall and narrow' appears, always choose 'Sparkling wine'!
