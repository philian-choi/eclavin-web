# Episode 021: Chardonnay (The Winemaker's Canvas) - White Grape Variety

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of the typical flavor changes in Chardonnay based on the climate where it is grown?

A. It always has only red fruit aromas like strawberry and cherry.
B. It has green apple flavors in cool climates and tropical fruit flavors like pineapple in warm climates.
C. It has no aroma at all and is always produced by injecting carbon dioxide gas.
D. It has high tannin and is always produced as a very astringent red wine.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. It has green apple flavors in cool climates and tropical fruit flavors like pineapple in warm climates.

Explanation: Chardonnay is a 'chameleon-like' variety.

Its flavors change dramatically depending on the climate: in cool climates, it shows aromas of apple or pear; in moderate climates, stone fruits like peach; and in warm climates, tropical fruit aromas such as pineapple, banana, or mango.

Additionally, depending on the winemaker's techniques (oak, malolactic fermentation), notes of butter or vanilla can be added.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Chardonnay is a classic example of the 'Harmony of Climate and Winemaking':

1.  Climate Spectrum:
    - Cool: Green apple, Pear, Lemon (e.g., Chablis)
    - Moderate: Peach, Melon (e.g., Burgundy)
    - Warm: Pineapple, Banana, Mango (e.g., California)

2.  Winemaking Touch:
    - Oak: Adds aromas of vanilla and toast.
    - MLF (Malolactic Fermentation): Adds a creamy texture like butter or yogurt.
    - Lees: Adds savory notes like bread dough or biscuit.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid the assumption that "All Chardonnay tastes like butter."
Many styles of Chardonnay, like Chablis, do not use oak and are fresh and crisp.
[Tip]: If the word 'Chardonnay' appears in the exam, immediately think of 'Climate Influence.'
The magic of aromas changing from apple to pineapple is the core of Chardonnay.
