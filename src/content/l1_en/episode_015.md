# Episode 015: Fermentation Science (The Alchemy of Wine) - Process

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. What are the three main products created when yeast consumes the sugar in grape juice during the winemaking process?

A. Water, Oxygen, and Nitrogen
B. Alcohol, Carbon Dioxide (CO2), and Heat
C. Sugar, Vitamins, and Minerals
D. Color, Tannin, and Maturation Aromas

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Alcohol, Carbon Dioxide (CO2), and Heat

Explanation: Fermentation is a chemical reaction through which yeast obtains energy by consuming sugar.

In this process, alcohol is produced, turning the juice into wine, while carbon dioxide gas and heat are also generated as byproducts of the reaction.

In red winemaking, this generated heat also helps in the extraction of color from the skins.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Fermentation is the sacred formula that marks the birth of wine:

1.  The Equation: Sugar + Yeast → Alcohol + Carbon Dioxide (CO2) + Heat

2.  The Role of Yeast: Yeast, either naturally occurring on grape skins or added by the winemaker, breaks down the sugar to create energy.

3.  Stopping Fermentation: If the yeast consumes all the sugar, the wine is Dry. If the process is halted prematurely, the wine remains Sweet.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Do not assume that "Carbon dioxide is only necessary for sparkling wines."
All fermentations produce CO2; the only difference is that still wines let it escape, while sparkling wines trap it inside the bottle.

[Tip]: If the word 'Fermentation' appears in the exam, immediately think of the 'Alcohol, CO2, and Heat' trio.
This formula is the foundation of all wine science.
