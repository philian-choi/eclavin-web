# Episode 048: Health & Safety (The Responsible Professional - Final Prep)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. When recommending responsible drinking as a wine professional, what is the most important thing to emphasize?

A. Drinking as much wine as possible every day.
B. Complying with low-risk drinking guidelines recommended by the government and not drinking alcohol if necessary.
C. Driving immediately after drinking wine.
D. Regular drinking of wine by pregnant women.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Complying with low-risk drinking guidelines recommended by the government and not drinking alcohol if necessary.

Explanation: WSET title holders should not only share the pleasure of wine but also social responsibility and health.

Excessive alcohol consumption can cause health problems such as liver disease and cardiovascular disease, and especially alcohol intake by pregnant women can result in Fetal Alcohol Syndrome.

It is a professional quality to adjust the amount consumed at one time, consume enough water, and never drive or operate machinery after drinking.

---

## [Expert Concept - Final Prep]

These are guidelines for responsible drinking to protect your health.

1.  Standard Units: A unit for measuring the amount of pure alcohol. It is a standard for accurately recognizing one's limits.

2.  Health Risks: Long-term heavy drinking can cause serious harm to physical and mental health.

3.  Safety First: 
    - Drinking & Driving: Even small amounts of alcohol can impair judgment. Absolutely forbidden! 
    - Pregnancy: To protect the safety of the fetus, drinking should be completely avoided.

4.  Responsible Service: In service environments like restaurants, it is a legal and ethical responsibility not to sell more liquor to intoxicated people.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Avoid thinking that "drinking wine is beneficial to health in large quantities because it is made from fruit."
The risk of alcohol always exists.
[Tip]: In an exam, if 'Guidelines,' 'Pregnancy,' and 'Operating machinery' keywords appear, connect them with 'Safety'!
