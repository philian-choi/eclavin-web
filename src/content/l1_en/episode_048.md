# Episode 048: Health & Safety (The Responsible Professional - Final Prep)

## [WSET L1 Practice Question]

Q. As a wine professional recommending 'Responsible Drinking', what is the most important point to emphasize?

A. Drinking as much wine as possible every day.
B. Following the government-recommended 'Low-risk drinking guidelines' and not drinking alcohol when necessary.
C. Driving immediately after drinking wine.
D. Pregnant women drinking wine regularly.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Following the government-recommended 'Low-risk drinking guidelines' and not drinking alcohol when necessary.

Explanation: Holders of the WSET designation must convey not only the joy of wine but also health and social responsibility. 

Excessive alcohol consumption can cause health problems such as liver disease and cardiovascular disease, and in particular, drinking by pregnant women can lead to Fetal Alcohol Syndrome. 

It is the mark of a professional to control the amount consumed at one time, drink plenty of water, and absolutely never drive or operate machinery after drinking.

---

## [Expert Concept]

These are responsible drinking guidelines that protect your health.

1.  Standard Units: A unit for measuring the amount of pure alcohol. It serves as a standard for accurately recognizing one's limits.
2.  Health Risks: Long-term heavy drinking can cause severe harm to physical and mental health.
3.  Safety First:
    - Drinking & Driving: Even a small amount of alcohol clouds judgment. Absolutely forbidden!
    - Pregnancy: You must abstain perfectly for the safety of the fetus.
4.  Responsible Service: It is a legal and ethical responsibility not to sell more alcohol to intoxicated guests in service environments like restaurants.

---

## [Exam Traps & Success Tips]

[Trap]: Thinking "Wine is made from fruit, so drinking a lot is good for your health" is incorrect. 
You must keep in mind that the dangers of alcohol always exist.
[Tip]: In the exam, if the keywords 'Guidelines', 'Pregnancy', and 'Operating machinery' appear, connect them with 'Safety'!
