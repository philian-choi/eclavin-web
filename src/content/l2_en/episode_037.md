# Episode 037: Other Global Reds (Malbec, Carmenère, Pinotage - Deep Dive)

## [WSET L2 Practice Question]

Q. Which of the following is a signature grape of Chile, characterized by thick skins, full body, blackberry fruit, and notes of green bell pepper and herbs?

A. Chardonnay
B. Carmenère
C. Malbec
D. Pinot Noir

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Carmenère

Explanation: Carmenère is the iconic flagship variety of Chile.

It has Thick Skins and generates a powerful Full Body with high tannins.

Its most distinctive trait is the green, herbaceous profile of Green Bell Pepper and Cool Herbs, especially when not fully ripened.

---

## [Core Theory Master]

Global red varieties define the national identity of their respective regions:

1.  Malbec: 
    - Argentina (Mendoza): The heart and soul of Argentina.
    - Characteristics: Deep Color, Full Body, Medium-to-High Tannin. 
    - Aroma: Concentrated blackberry, red plum, and elegant Violet.

2.  Carmenère: 
    - Chile (Central Valley): A sun-loving variety that needs a long growing season.
    - Characteristics: High Tannin, Full Body, Intense structure.
    - Aroma: Blackberry fruit layered with signature Green Bell Pepper and herbaceous spices.

3.  Pinotage: 
    - South Africa (Western Cape): A unique crossing of Pinot Noir and Cinsault.
    - Characteristics: High Tannin, Deep Color. 
    - Aroma: Red berry fruit combined with distinct notes of Coffee, Smoke, and Earth.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Do not think Malbec should have herbal notes. "Green bell pepper" is the exclusive signature of 'Carmenère' in the L2 curriculum.
[Tip]: Link 'Argentina', 'Blackberry', and 'Violet' to 'Malbec'.

Deep floral elegance is Malbec’s badge of honor.
