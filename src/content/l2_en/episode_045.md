# Episode 045: Global Spirits Tour (Brandy, Whiskey, Tequila - Deep Dive)

## [WSET L2 Practice Question]

**Q. Which of the following is the most accurate description of Tequila, Mexico’s iconic spirit?**

A. It is always made from grain and produced primarily using Column Stills.
B. It is made from the **Blue Agave** plant and is characterized by distinct **Herbaceous (grass)** and **Peppery** flavors.
C. It is produced by adding large amounts of refined sugar during the distillation process.
D. It is a typical sparkling distilled spirit with high carbonation.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: B. It is made from the Blue Agave plant and is characterized by distinct Herbaceous (grass) and Peppery flavors.**

**Explanation**: **Tequila** must be produced from the heart (Piña) of the 'Blue Agave' plant, which is steamed and then distilled. In its unaged **Blanco** form, the fresh grass and spicy pepper notes are most evident. Varieties like **Reposado** or **Añejo** are aged in oak to add notes of vanilla, caramel, and subtle smoke.

---

## [Expert Concept]

Global spirits are a masterclass in raw material expression and aging:

1.  **Brandy (Grapes)**: 
    - **Cognac**: From the Cognac region in France. Twice-distilled in Pot Stills and aged in oak. (Aromas: Floral, fruity, nutty).
2.  **Whiskey (Grains)**: 
    - **Scotch**: Made from Malt (barley) or grains in Scotland. Often features 'Peat' smoke notes.
    - **Bourbon**: An American whiskey made primarily from corn (at least 51%). Known for sweet vanilla and coconut notes from charred oak.
3.  **Rum (Sugar Cane)**: 
    - Made from molasses or sugar cane juice. Ranges from Light Rum (neutral) to Dark Rum (heavy, toffee).
4.  **Vodka & Gin**: 
    - **Vodka**: Neutral spirit with minimal raw material aroma.
    - **Gin**: Neutral spirit flavored with botanicals, primarily **Juniper Berry**.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not think Tequila is made from a cactus. It is made from the **'Agave'** plant, which is actually part of the lily family.
**[Tip]**: Link **'Agave'** + **'Grass/Herbaceous'** to Tequila. It is the liquid signature of the Mexican landscape.
