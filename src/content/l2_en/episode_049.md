# Episode 049: Exam Strategy & High-Level Traps (The Strategist - Final Deep Dive)

## [WSET L2 Practice Question]

**Q. When completing the WSET Level 2 exam (50 questions in 60 minutes), what is the most efficient strategy for success?**

A. Spend at least 5 minutes carefully reconsidering every single question.
B. Quickly answer questions you know and use the **Process of Elimination** (PoE) to remove impossible options for difficult questions.
C. Choose the longest option as it is most likely the correct technical answer.
D. Finish the entire exam within 10 minutes and leave the room immediately.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

**Answer: B. Quickly answer questions you know and use the Process of Elimination (PoE) to remove impossible options for difficult questions.**

**Explanation**: The **WSET exam** tests your ability to connect 'Core Concepts' rather than obscure trivia. For example, if a question mentions a 'Cool' climate, you can immediately eliminate answers like 'Black Cherry' or 'High Alcohol.' Aim for about 1 minute per question, mark those you are unsure of, and use the remaining time for a calm, strategic review.

---

## [Expert Concept - Final Check]

Here is a list of the 'High-Level Traps' often set by examiners:

1.  **Confusing PDO Names**: 
    - **Pouilly-Fuissé**: Burgundy Chardonnay (White).
    - **Pouilly-Fumé**: Loire Sauvignon Blanc (White).
    - They sound similar, but the grape and region are completely different!
2.  **Similar Brandings & Methods**: 
    - **Cava**: Spanish Traditional Method sparkling.
    - **Prosecco**: Italian Tank Method sparkling.
    - Watch for questions that swap the grape varieties or production methods.
3.  **Label Terminology Traps**: 
    - **Reserva** (Spain): Strict legal aging requirements.
    - **Riserva** (Italy): Italian specific aging laws.
    - **Reserve** (New World): Often has no strict legal meaning; used as a marketing term.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

**[Trap]**: Do not think high-quality wines are only made in 'Hot' climates. Varieties like Pinot Noir and Chardonnay reach their pinnacle in **'Cool'** or moderate climates.
**[Tip]**: When reading a question, always look for the three pillars: **'Climate'**, **'Grape Variety'**, and **'Region'**!
