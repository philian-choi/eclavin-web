# Episode 049: Exam Strategy & High-Level Traps (The Strategist - Final Deep Dive)

## [WSET L2 Practice Question]

Q. When solving 50 questions in 60 minutes for the WSET Level 2 exam, what is the most efficient strategy?

A. Spend more than 5 minutes on a single question.
B. Solve the questions you know quickly, and use the Process of Elimination for others.
C. Pick the longest answer choice without reading.
D. Finish the exam in 10 minutes and leave the room.

---

## [Answer & Explanation]

Answer: B. Solve the questions you know quickly, and use the Process of Elimination for others.

Explanation: The WSET Level 2 exam requires you to answer 50 multiple-choice questions in 60 minutes. Because time can be tight, the wisest strategy is to quickly solve the questions you know instantly, and invest the remaining time on the trickier ones. In particular, the 'Process of Elimination' is highly effective in WSET exams. Compare the core keywords (grape variety, climate, country) in each option and logically approach the question by eliminating the definitely wrong answers first.

---

## [Core Theory Master]

High-scoring strategies you must remember for the WSET Level 2 exam:

1.  Keyword Matching: If you see 'Australia' and 'Mint' in a question, immediately connect it to Coonawarra Cabernet Sauvignon. If you see 'South Africa' and 'Coffee', link it to Pinotage.
2.  Distinguishing Similar Names: Questions distinguishing wines with similar names but completely different grapes and styles—like Pouilly-Fuissé (Burgundy Chardonnay) vs Pouilly-Fumé (Loire Sauvignon Blanc)—appear in almost every exam.
3.  Memorizing the SAT (Systematic Approach to Tasting): Questions asking for objective criteria in color terms (Lemon, Gold, Amber) or aroma development (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary) are easy points if you memorize the SAT table perfectly.

---

## [Exam Tip & Trap]

[Trap]: Relying on baseless guessing tricks like "The longest answer is always correct" or stereotypes like "Expensive wine is only made in hot climates" will easily lead you to wrong answers.
[Tip]: When faced with a difficult question, don't panic. Look for the three hidden clues in the question: 'Climate', 'Grape Variety', and 'Region'. Use these keywords to deduce the answer logically.
